The Loci Method

Loci or the memory journey, uses specific physical locations that are familiar to the participant. Assigning unfamiliar information to the information that your brain already has stored naturally.

1. Think of a place you know well, such as your own house.
2. Visualize a series of locations in the place in logical order.
3. Place each item that you want to remember at one of the locations.
4. When you want to remember items, simply visualize your house and go through it room by room in your mind.

Mnemonics

Have you ever counted family members coming to dinner on your fingers? Have you ever saved friends names in your cellphone by a specific trait, like tall tyler or fast frank? Than you have used the mnemonic method. Mnemonic or a memory device is an umbrella term for techniques that assist with retrieval of information.

Mnemonic systems are simple to implicate. Attach a song to some cooking instructions or write a poem to remember your friends birthday. The options are endless.

Storytelling

Storytelling is all the instructions you need for this method. Try and share your newly stored knowledge with a friend or even your goldfish. The best test of any information is being able to transfer that knowledge to another.

Chunking

Chunking is a valuable method in the retention of information. If you have ever forgotten a phone number because there are too many numbers, maybe try chunking. Instead of saying 1 - 2 - 3 - 4, try 12 - 34. This is chunking. Taking individual pieces of information and grouping them into larger units. By grouping each piece into a large whole, you can improve the amount of information you can remember.
